# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and ensure the team adheres to agile practices. They serve the team by coaching agile principles and shielding developers from external disruptions.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that prevent the team from delivering
- Coach the team on Scrum and agile best practices
- Shield the team from external interruptions and scope creep
- Track team velocity and help improve estimation accuracy

### Goals
- Foster a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Continuously improve team delivery process and efficiency
- Ensure sprint goals are achievable and consistently met

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and impediment escalations
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation notes
- Agile metrics reports (velocity, burndown)

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline, dependencies, and escalations; the Scrum Master focuses on process while the PM focuses on delivery commitments.
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on backlog readiness and sprint goals to ensure stories are well-defined before each sprint.
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices, removes blockers, and creates a safe environment for continuous improvement.

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and document requirements, bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams, and maintain traceability throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business and functional requirements
- Translate business needs into clear technical specifications and user stories
- Maintain requirements traceability from business goals to acceptance criteria
- Facilitate workshops and interviews with stakeholders to surface needs and constraints
- Review deliverables to ensure they meet the original business intent

### Goals
- Ensure complete, unambiguous requirements that reduce rework
- Bridge the gap between business stakeholders and engineering teams
- Maintain alignment between business objectives and technical solutions

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Stakeholder workshop notes and decision logs
- Traceability matrices linking requirements to deliverables

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates to refine and prioritize requirements, ensuring business goals translate into actionable backlog items.
- **Project Manager**: Provides requirements clarity to support accurate planning, estimation, and scope management.
- **Developers**: Clarifies acceptance criteria and business rules to resolve ambiguities during implementation.

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers lead user research, interaction design, and prototyping to ensure that product features are customer-centric, intuitive, and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, usability tests, and interviews to understand customer needs
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define interaction patterns, information architecture, and visual design guidelines
- Collaborate with engineering to ensure designs are feasible and implemented accurately
- Iterate on designs based on user feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs
- Reduce user friction and support high feature adoption
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design files, wireframes, and prototypes shared via design tools
- Usability test reports and user research summaries
- Design critique sessions and handoff documentation for developers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partners on defining the user problem and validating that proposed solutions meet customer needs.
- **Developers**: Provides design specifications and participates in handoff reviews to ensure accurate implementation.
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates design milestone timelines to align with sprint and release schedules.

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep domain knowledge and specialized expertise to inform decisions, validate requirements, and ensure deliverables meet real-world constraints and standards.

### Responsibilities
- Advise on domain-specific feasibility, constraints, and best practices
- Review and validate requirements, designs, and deliverables for accuracy
- Support the team in understanding regulatory, compliance, or industry-specific considerations
- Participate in key ceremonies or reviews where domain input is critical
- Serve as a resource for resolving domain ambiguities during execution

### Goals
- Ensure project outputs are accurate, compliant, and fit for purpose in the domain
- Reduce risk by surfacing domain constraints early in the project lifecycle
- Build team knowledge of specialized domain areas over time

### Typical Communication
- Review comments on requirements documents and design artifacts
- Domain briefings and knowledge-sharing sessions
- Ad-hoc consultation during planning and execution phases

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Manager**: Validates that product requirements and success metrics align with domain realities and standards.
- **Project Manager**: Highlights domain-specific risks and constraints that should be reflected in the project plan.
- **Developers**: Provides guidance on domain rules, data models, or compliance requirements during implementation.
- **Business Analyst**: Collaborates to ensure requirements accurately capture domain-specific nuances and constraints.
